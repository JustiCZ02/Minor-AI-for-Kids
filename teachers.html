60-minute lesson flow

A simple structure you can adjust in real time.

  1. Warm-up: quick AI examples students already use.
  2. Module walkthrough: what AI is and how it learns.
  3. Deepfake practice: spot clues in images or clips.
  4. AI Mentor chat: discuss choices and consequences.
  5. Wrap-up: privacy takeaways and reflection.

Learning goals

By the end, students should be able to do the following.

Explain AI simply

Describe how AI uses data and patterns to make predictions.

Spot risks

Recognize privacy risks and misleading AI outputs.

Question results

Check sources and challenge confident-sounding answers.

Make safer choices

Decide when to share data and when to say no.

Discussion starters

Use these to guide small-group or class conversations.

Everyday AI

  • Where do you notice AI when you use your phone?
  • What parts of your feed feel most personalized?
  • What would you change about how apps recommend content?

Data and privacy

  • When is it okay to share location, and when is it not?
  • What data should apps never ask for?
  • How can you check what an app is collecting?

Trust and fairness

  • How could an AI be unfair? How could we fix that?
  • What is a deepfake and why does it matter?
  • How can we verify information before sharing it?

Classroom norms

Keep the conversation safe and respectful.

  • Assume good intent and ask clarifying questions.
  • Use examples that are age-appropriate and respectful.
  • Focus on choices and habits, not on blaming anyone.
  • Remind students that real people are affected by AI systems.
