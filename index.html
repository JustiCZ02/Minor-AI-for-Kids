Classroom plan

AI Data Camp helps students understand how AI shows up in their daily apps and how to protect their data.

Suggested 45-60 minute lesson:

  • 5 min: warm-up questions about AI.
  • 10 min: explore ‚ÄúWhat is AI?‚Äù together.
  • 20 min: students read modules in pairs on devices.
  • 10 min: play the quiz as a class.
  • 10 min: discuss data & privacy choices.

Learning objectives:

  • Students can explain in simple words what AI is.
  • Students can give examples of AI in daily life.
  • Students understand basic risks of sharing data.
  • Students know at least 3 tips to stay safe online.

Class Discussion Starters

  • Where do you notice AI when you use your phone?
  • How can data help an app improve? How can it hurt?
  • When is it okay to share your location, and when is it not?
  • What would you do if an app asked for contacts it does not need?
  • How could an AI be unfair? How could we fix that?
  • What is one habit you can change today to stay safer online?

About AI Data Camp

This site is a prototype from a student project. It is designed for teens 12-15 years old to grow digital citizenship and AI literacy. Use it to explore how AI tools work and how to keep your data safe.

Who is this for?

  • Secondary school students (12-15)
  • Teachers and schools
  • Parents who want to talk about AI with their children

What you can learn

  • What AI is and where you see it
  • How data trains AI models
  • Why privacy matters
  • Ways to stay safe and think critically
  • How AI shapes feeds, filters, and games
